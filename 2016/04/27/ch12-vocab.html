Term Definition
Love language Concept that people communicate and receive love through specific acts (Broken down into five categories)
Words of affirmation Love language that relies on emotional support or encouragement
Quality time Love language which is expressed by being around the partner
Receiving Gifts Love language which in which partner feels love best when reveiving a gift
Acts of Service Love language in which partner feels love from spouse doing work for them
Physical Touch Love language in which partner feels love from contact with spouse
godly family family was microcosm of the larger society. moral instruction was more important than building child’s self-esteem
democratic family family which stemmed from work moving outside the home. Family became a protective shell
companionate family celebrate personality. Smaller family size. The child will lead a better life than the parents
family A network of people who live together over long periods of time bound by ties of marriage, blood, or commitment, legal or otherwise
provide care, socialization, intellectual development, recreation, and emotional support Celebrate personality. Smaller family size. The child will lead a better life than the parents.
transmission-family the transmitter of cultural values from generation to generation
family communication climate framework that defines appropriate behavior and directs communication goals
Conformity parental power to ensure control, agreement, and appearance of harmony
conversation open expression of ideas. Values and fosters self-expression.
clear messages messages spoken plainly and content is understood (Ex: I’m disappointed you forgot to take out the trash without me having to remind you)
masked messages messages are muddied or vague (Ex: You kids are so lazy)
instrumental communication exchange of factual information that enables individuals to fulfill common family functions.(I need you to pick me up from soccer practice.)
affective communication the way family members share emotions with one another.(I’m angry that you were the only parent not at the game)
